# RHOAI Quality Verification Checklist

This prompt is used to validate RHOAI course content against quality standards.

## Content Analysis Task

Analyze the provided AsciiDoc content against RHOAI standards. Provide:

1. **Overall Readiness Score** (0-100)
   - STRONG (90-100): Ready for publication
   - GOOD (75-89): Minor revisions (1-2 hours)
   - ADEQUATE (60-74): Significant improvements needed
   - WEAK (<60): Major revisions required

2. **Category Scores** (breakdown by component)
3. **Issue List** (line numbers, severity, specific feedback)
4. **Suggestions** (actionable improvements)
5. **Estimated Effort** (hours to reach GOOD/STRONG)

---

## Scoring Categories

### 1. Structure (25%)

Check for required document structure:

- [ ] Document attributes present (:time_estimate:, :imagesdir:, etc.)
- [ ] Title present (= format)
- [ ] Reading time estimate shown
- [ ] Lead paragraph [.lead] present
- [ ] Objective:: definition present (or equivalent for content type)
- [ ] Main content sections present
- [ ] "What's Next" section present (dedicated == heading)
- [ ] Proper heading hierarchy (no skipped levels: h1→h3)
- [ ] Navigation references valid (if cross-references)

**Scoring**:
- All present and correct: 100
- Minor issues (1-2 missing/wrong): 80
- Multiple issues (3+ missing): 50
- Major structural problems: < 50

---

### 2. RHOAI Style (35%)

This is the core of RHOAI identity. Score each component:

#### A. Lead Opener (30% of style subscore = 10.5% of overall)

The [.lead] paragraph must:
- [ ] Be present ([.lead] block)
- [ ] Use bold (*text*)
- [ ] Be italic (included in bold: *text*)
- [ ] Contain single declarative sentence
- [ ] Establish engineering authority (not "learn about", not vague)
- [ ] Not be a question
- [ ] Differ from section title
- [ ] Connect to content without previewing it

**Examples**:
- ✅ "Production deployment requires governance, not just kubectl apply."
- ✅ "GPU sharing requires fairness enforced by schedulers, not hope."
- ❌ "This section covers production deployment."
- ❌ "Have you thought about governance?"
- ❌ "We will learn about deployment."

**Scoring**:
- Strong (all criteria met): 28-30
- Good (meets most, minor issues): 24-27
- Adequate (present but weak): 18-23
- Poor (missing or very weak): <18

#### B. Business Narrative (30% of style subscore = 10.5% of overall)

Check for clear business value connection:

- [ ] Business context paragraph present
- [ ] Located within first 100 words of content
- [ ] Mentions specific business concern:
  - [ ] Cost/ROI
  - [ ] Compliance/governance
  - [ ] Efficiency/speed
  - [ ] Reliability/errors
- [ ] Includes specific numbers where relevant ($X, Y% improvement, etc.)
- [ ] Uses platform engineer perspective ("A platform engineer must...")
- [ ] Uses contrast pattern (developer vs. platform, shadow vs. factory)
- [ ] Business scenario (if provided) integrated naturally
- [ ] Doesn't just describe features (must explain WHY)

**Examples**:
- ✅ "A developer deploys models with kubectl. A platform engineer ensures that deployment follows approval gates, leaves audit trails, and integrates with cost tracking."
- ✅ "GPU costs are $50K/month. Without governance, one team monopolizes the cluster, and others wait in queues."
- ❌ "In this section, you will learn about governance."
- ❌ "Governance is important for enterprises."

**Scoring**:
- Strong (clear business value, platform perspective, specificity): 28-30
- Good (business value clear, mostly specific): 24-27
- Adequate (business mentioned but weak): 18-23
- Poor (missing or vague): <18

#### C. Definition Lists (20% of style subscore = 7% of overall)

Check for proper use of definition list syntax (::):

- [ ] Objective:: used correctly
- [ ] Key concepts use :: format where appropriate
- [ ] Not using bullets when :: format should apply
- [ ] At least 3-5 definitions in presentation/lab
- [ ] Proper syntax throughout

**Scoring**:
- Excellent (multiple, proper syntax): 18-20
- Good (several present, mostly correct): 14-17
- Adequate (some present, minor syntax issues): 10-13
- Poor (missing or incorrect): <10

#### D. What's Next Section (20% of style subscore = 7% of overall)

Check the "What's Next" section:

- [ ] Present (dedicated == section)
- [ ] Not generic ("Next we'll learn..." ❌)
- [ ] Explains what capability is enabled by completing this section
- [ ] Shows business connection to next topic
- [ ] Motivates progression without just previewing content
- [ ] Flows logically from this section to next

**Examples**:
- ✅ "Now that you've deployed safely, the next section shows how to monitor cost and catch errors before users do."
- ❌ "In the next section, you will learn about monitoring."
- ❌ "The next topic is monitoring models."

**Scoring**:
- Strong (enables thinking, business connection): 18-20
- Good (forward-looking, mostly business-focused): 14-17
- Adequate (present but generic): 10-13
- Poor (missing or very generic): <10

---

### 3. Style Guide (20%)

Check for adherence to RHOAI style rules:

#### Prohibited Terms
Search and flag these words:
- "robust" → Too generic, be specific
- "powerful" → Show capability instead
- "leverage" → Use "use" or "apply"
- "synergy" → Find the actual benefit
- "game-changer" → Describe the change
- "cutting-edge" → Be specific about what's new
- "best-in-class" → Compared to what?
- "innovative" → What specifically is new?

#### Capitalization & Grammar
- [ ] Headlines in sentence case (not Title Case)
  - ✅ "Understanding GPU memory requirements"
  - ❌ "Understanding GPU Memory Requirements"
- [ ] Product names correct: "Red Hat OpenShift AI" (first), then "OpenShift AI" or "RHOAI"
- [ ] No "the" before product names ("OpenShift AI" not "the OpenShift AI")
- [ ] Oxford commas in lists ("models, inference, and deployment")
- [ ] Numbers < 10 spelled out ("five nodes"), >= 10 as numerals ("12 instances")
- [ ] No unnecessary capitalization

#### Language Quality
- [ ] Active voice (avoid passive)
  - ✅ "You deploy" not "It is deployed"
- [ ] Direct address (use "you")
- [ ] Specific language (numbers, concrete examples)
- [ ] Inclusive language (no gendered pronouns, accessible examples)
- [ ] Proper punctuation and grammar

**Scoring**:
- Clean (no issues): 20
- Minor issues (1-2): 16-19
- Several issues (3-5): 10-15
- Major issues (6+): <10

---

### 4. Technical Accuracy (15%)

For code-heavy content (labs, technical presentations):

#### Code Blocks
- [ ] Source type specified ([source,bash], [source,yaml], etc.)
- [ ] Correct type used:
  - [ ] Commands: [source,bash] or [source,console]
  - [ ] Configuration: [source,yaml]
  - [ ] Scripts: [source,python], [source,bash], etc.
  - [ ] Output: plain text (no source type)
- [ ] Syntax is correct (would parse/run)
- [ ] Copy-paste ready (no placeholders without context)

#### For Labs Specifically
- [ ] Verification steps included after major tasks
- [ ] Expected output shown (not just commands)
- [ ] Success criteria clear (✓ checks, "ready", "success")
- [ ] Commands tested in target environment (assumption validation)
- [ ] Prerequisites clearly stated
- [ ] Troubleshooting present (if --include-troubleshooting)

#### General Technical
- [ ] Product names and versions accurate
- [ ] No misleading or wrong technical claims
- [ ] API references and command syntax match current version
- [ ] Assumptions about environment clearly stated

**Scoring**:
- All accurate and complete: 15
- Minor issues (1-2 gaps, small errors): 11-14
- Several issues (3-5, some incomplete): 6-10
- Major issues (wrong commands, missing output): <6

---

### 5. Accessibility (5%)

- [ ] Images have alt text (if present)
- [ ] Links are descriptive (not "click here")
- [ ] Heading hierarchy is correct
- [ ] Content structure clear for screen readers
- [ ] Code examples have context
- [ ] Tables have headers and summaries

**Scoring**:
- All present: 5
- Minor issues (1 missing): 4
- Several issues (2-3): 2-3
- Major issues: <2

---

## Calculation Formula

```
Overall Score = (Structure × 0.25) + (RHOAI_Style × 0.35) +
                (Style_Guide × 0.20) + (Technical × 0.15) +
                (Accessibility × 0.05)

Where:
- Structure: 0-100
- RHOAI_Style: 0-100 (comprised of 4 weighted components)
- Style_Guide: 0-100
- Technical: 0-100
- Accessibility: 0-100
```

---

## Issue Classification

For each issue found:

**Line Number**: Where in the document

**Issue Description**: What's wrong

**Context**: 1-2 sentence explanation of why this matters

**Suggestion**: How to fix it, with example if helpful

**Severity**:
- **ERROR**: Blocks publication (missing critical element, broken code)
- **WARNING**: Should fix before publishing (quality issue, affects readiness)
- **SUGGESTION**: Optional improvement (nice-to-have, doesn't block)

---

## Readiness Assessment

Based on overall score:

### STRONG (90-100)
- Lead opener excellent
- Business narrative clear and specific
- Structure complete and well-organized
- Code examples verified
- What's Next is business-focused
- **Action**: Ready for publication

### GOOD (75-89)
- Core elements present
- Minor style inconsistencies
- Business connection could be stronger
- Code examples mostly verified
- **Action**: Minor revisions (1-2 hours), then publish

### ADEQUATE (60-74)
- Structure present but weak
- Business narrative missing or vague
- Code examples need verification
- What's Next is too generic
- **Action**: Significant improvements needed (2-4 hours)

### WEAK (<60)
- Missing critical elements
- Little or no business narrative
- Code may have errors
- Structure is incomplete
- **Action**: Major revision required (4+ hours)

## Special Cases

### For Presentations
- Should have 2-3 major sections (== headings)
- Should discuss trade-offs
- Should include "Common Mistakes" section
- Should have 4-6 key concepts
- Time estimate should be 15-20 minutes

### For Labs
- Should have 2-4 numbered tasks
- Each task should have verification steps
- Prerequisites should be clearly listed
- Troubleshooting section is expected
- Should have learning outcomes checklist
- Time estimate should be 30-60 minutes

### For Quizzes
- Should have 5 scenario-based questions
- Each question should have 3-4 multiple choice options
- Should include explanations for correct answers
- Should provide scoring guidance
- Time estimate should be 10-15 minutes
