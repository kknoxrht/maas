= The GPU Silo Crisis

_Estimated reading time: *{time_estimate} minutes*._

[.lead]
*Expensive GPU hardware trapped in team silos creates idle capacity in some departments while high-priority projects stall in others.*

Objective::
Understand the business and operational problems created by fragmented GPU infrastructure and how the Models-as-a-Service "Centralized Model Hub" solves them.

== The Problem: Dedicated Accelerators

Organizations currently face a "GPU Silo" crisis where expensive, limited hardware is trapped within individual teams. This leads to simultaneous idle capacity and project bottlenecks.

A platform engineer must allocate $200,000 worth of GPU hardware fairly across multiple teams and projects.

Without centralized orchestration, teams request and manage their own dedicated GPUs. This creates:

* **Wasted Resources**: GPU hardware sitting idle in Team A while Team B's projects are queued
* **Shadow AI**: Multiple teams downloading and deploying the same models independently
* **Zero Visibility**: No audit logs, no PII filtering, no usage tracking
* **Debugging Tax**: Data scientists waste time on model deployment tasks such as managing CUDA drivers and Kubernetes instead of model tuning

== The Solution: Centralized Model Hub

The Models-as-a-Service (MaaS) capability transforms GPU infrastructure from dedicated, single-team clusters into a consumable model backplane available to the entire enterprise.

Central IT::
Manages the inference strategy, hardware allocation, and governance policies

Lines of Business::
Consume AI capabilities via tokens; treating model inference as a metered, on-demand internal service

Platform Engineers::
Configure tiers, rate limits, and observability dashboards

Developers::
Focus on applications, not infrastructure

== Business Impact

Without MaaS::
* Redundant model deployments waste storage and compute
* No audit trail for compliance requirements
* Cannot track usage or forecast future needs
* Teams compete for scarce GPU resources

With MaaS::
* Centralized model catalog eliminates redundancy
* Complete audit logs and PII filtering via API gateway
* Token-based consumption enables accurate chargeback
* Fair resource allocation prevents team conflicts

== The Transition Framework

Moving from silos to centralized serving requires:

1. **Generic GPU Enablement** - The foundation (drivers, operators, hardware certification)
2. **MaaS Layer** - The wiring (KServe, Kuadrant, Gateway API)
3. **Governance Framework** - The controls (tiers, rate limits, quotas)
4. **Token Delivery Model** - The consumption interface (OpenAI-compatible APIs)
