= ChocoTech Industries

== Your role and mission

In this course, you will perform the role of a Platform Engineer at ChocoTech Industries, a modern chocolate manufacturing company, and you've been assigned to solve a critical infrastructure challenge affecting multiple business units.

ChocoTech has teams across Quality Control, R&D, Marketing, and Supply Chain all trying to leverage AI for their operations. However, each team purchased their own GPU infrastructure, leading to the "GPU Silo Crisis."

**Your assignment**: Design and implement a Models as a Service (MaaS) platform on Red Hat OpenShift AI to transform fragmented GPU resources into a centralized, governed model hub.

**The objective**: Enable lines of business to access generative AI models through a self-service catalog with proper governance, eliminating resource waste and accelerating business innovation.

== Success criteria for your mission

By the end of this QuickCourse, you'll have practical MaaS implementation skills to address ChocoTech's GPU infrastructure challenges:

* Understand the business impact of the GPU silo crisis and articulate how MaaS prevents "Shadow AI" → **eliminate redundant deployments and resource waste**
* Configure the MaaS platform through OpenShift AI operator CRDs → **establish centralized model serving infrastructure**
* Deploy models using distributed inference with llm-d and vLLM → **enable efficient GPU utilization across multiple accelerators**
* Implement governance tiers with rate limiting and quotas using Kuadrant → **ensure fair resource allocation and compliance**
* Generate usage metrics and chargeback reports → **provide visibility into model consumption and justify infrastructure costs**
* Integrate models into applications via OpenAI-compatible APIs → **enable developers to consume AI capabilities without infrastructure complexity**

**Technical outcome**: You'll have hands-on experience deploying and operating a production-ready MaaS platform that solves real business challenges.

**Business benefit**: Centralized model catalog with governed access that reduces costs, improves compliance, and accelerates AI application development.

== ChocoTech Industries' GPU infrastructure challenges

**The situation**: ChocoTech Industries has invested heavily in AI across multiple business units, but their decentralized approach created serious operational problems.

**Current state**: Four teams independently purchased and deployed GPU infrastructure:

* **Quality Control team**: Running computer vision models for defect detection on 2x A100 GPUs
* **R&D team**: Testing recipe optimization and flavor prediction models on 4x L40S GPUs
* **Marketing team**: Analyzing customer sentiment and demand forecasting on 2x L4 GPUs
* **Supply Chain team**: Running inventory prediction models on 2x L40S GPUs

**Operational pain points identified**:

* **GPU under-utilization and over-provisioning**: Quality Control's GPUs sit idle 60% of the time while R&D waits weeks for capacity, leading to unnecessary $200,000+ hardware purchases
* **Misallocation of engineering expertise**: Data scientists spend 40% of their time debugging CUDA drivers, Docker containers, and Kubernetes manifests instead of tuning models and analyzing data
* **Redundant model deployments**: All four teams independently downloaded and deployed Llama-3 8B, wasting 32GB of storage and compute capacity per deployment
* **Security "Black Boxes"**: Without a centralized API gateway, there is zero visibility into who is sending what data to which model, making audit logs and PII filtering nearly impossible to enforce for compliance
* **No way to track usage or forecast needs**: Finance cannot justify GPU infrastructure costs without visibility into model usage across users, applications, and different models

**The opportunity**: MaaS transforms GPU infrastructure from dedicated, single-team clusters into a consumable model backplane where centralized IT manages the inference strategy, hardware allocation, and governance policies while lines of business consume AI capabilities via tokens.

**Technical perspective**: "We need to transition from hardware management to token delivery. MaaS provides the wiring layer (KServe, Kuadrant, Gateway API) and governance framework (tiers, rate limits, quotas) to make this shift possible."

== Your vision of success

If MaaS implementation succeeds for ChocoTech, here's the potential impact:

**Immediate improvements** (short-term):

* **Eliminated redundancy**: Single Llama-3 8B deployment serves all four teams → 75% reduction in storage and compute waste
* **Fair resource allocation**: Tier-based quotas prevent Quality Control from monopolizing GPUs while R&D waits → balanced access across business units
* **Complete audit trail**: Every API request logged with user identity, model accessed, and tokens consumed → compliance requirements satisfied
* **Simplified developer experience**: Marketing team integrates models via OpenAI-compatible API in hours instead of weeks → faster time to value

**Strategic benefits** (longer-term):

* **Accurate chargeback**: Token-based consumption tracking enables Finance to bill departments based on actual usage → justify infrastructure investments
* **Centralized catalog governance**: Platform team controls which models are available, preventing "Shadow AI" deployments → standardization and security
* **Better resource utilization**: Intelligent routing and centralized inference maximize GPU efficiency → infrastructure costs reduced by 40-50%
* **Accelerated innovation**: Developers focus on building applications instead of managing infrastructure → increased team productivity and business agility

**Success metric**: ChocoTech can demonstrate to leadership that MaaS reduced GPU infrastructure costs, improved compliance posture, and accelerated AI application delivery across all business units.

→ Yes. Module 3 demonstrates deploying existing models from Hugging Face and custom model repositories using llm-d and vLLM serving runtimes

→ Module 4 covers governance policies including rate limiting, tier-based access control, and audit logging through the Kuadrant policy layer

**"How does this integrate with our current application development workflows?"**
→ Module 5 shows OpenAI-compatible API integration that works with existing LangChain, LlamaIndex, and custom application frameworks

**"Is MaaS adoption sustainable for our team with limited Kubernetes expertise?"**
→ The QuickCourse demonstrates operator-based configuration through OpenShift AI CRDs, minimizing low-level Kubernetes knowledge requirements while providing self-service capabilities via the dashboard
