= Module 1: The shift to centralized model serving

:source-highlighter: rouge
:toc: macro
:toclevels: 1

The CIO at ChocoTech Industries just called an emergency meeting. "We've invested over $500,000 in GPU infrastructure across four teams," she says, reviewing the quarterly report. "Yet our R&D team is waiting three weeks for capacity while Quality Control's GPUs sit idle 60% of the time. This doesn't make sense."

ChocoTech needs to transform their fragmented GPU infrastructure into a centralized, governed platform where all business units can access AI models efficiently. You've been tasked with implementing Models as a Service (MaaS) on Red Hat OpenShift AI to solve this challenge.

In this module, you'll understand the business problem MaaS solves and explore the technical architecture that makes centralized model serving possible.

toc::[]

== Learning objectives

By the end of this module, you'll be able to:

* Articulate the business and operational problems created by the GPU silo crisis
* Explain how MaaS transforms GPU infrastructure from hardware management to token delivery
* Map the three-layer MaaS architecture (KServe, Kuadrant, Gateway API) and their responsibilities
* Verify that the foundational components are installed and healthy in your OpenShift cluster
* Understand the complete request flow from API client through Gateway, policy enforcement, to model serving
